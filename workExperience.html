Operational CNC Wire Cutter

This machine, now in operation at Zaber Technologies, uses pneumatically controlled 5C collet chucks to pull in wire from the right side. An 8-inch abrasive cutting wheel, driven from behind, cuts the wire while it rotates. This controlled rotation ensures that any minimal burrs form at the center of the wire rather than along an edge.

Work in progress

The machine uses pneumatics to controll the 5C collet chucks, stepper motors to control the postion of the sub spindle, Cutter spindle, and rotation of the main and sub spindles. A Teknic motor is used to drive the abrasive cutting wheel.

Electrical enclosure

This was the first electrical enclosure I designed and built on my own. I also sourced and standardized some of the components. At the top is a Zaber 4-axis controller, followed by DIN rail-mounted 120V terminals, a 48V power supply, 5V terminals, and several relays. At the bottom is the power supply for the Teknic motor.

Pneumatics and Enclosure Panel

I 3D printed a panel to hold Molex Mini-Fit connectors, making prototyping incredibly easy. It worked very well and provided a fast, cost-effective solution. The pneumatics, sourced from Automation Direct, are connected to a simple manifold.

Rail Straightener and Driver

For this project, I designed and built multiple units to drive hardened wire through a wire straightener. The final result is a tunable wire straightener capable of correcting any deformation in the wire’s profile.

Wire Staighteners

I used a waterjet to manufacture the main body, which includes flexures for fine-tuning the straightener. Typically, a mechanical system would be used, but the flexures provide a simpler and effective solution for making minor adjustments.

Wire Drivers

The drive units use pneumatics to apply a constant, known force to the drive wheel, ensuring we achieve the necessary clamping force without over-clamping, which could permanently deform the wire’s profile. Most of the parts were manufactured from aluminum plate using the waterjet.
